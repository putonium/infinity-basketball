Policy

Code of Conduct

Infinity Basketball Club is committed to a positive, respectful environment for all players, coaches, families, and volunteers.

Player Expectations

All players participating in Infinity Basketball Club are expected to:

  • Treat teammates, opponents, coaches, officials, and spectators with respect at all times.
  • Arrive on time and prepared for practices and games.
  • Give maximum effort in every practice and game situation.
  • Accept coaching feedback positively and work to improve.
  • Support and encourage teammates regardless of performance.
  • Uphold the values of the club both on and off the court.

Parent & Guardian Expectations

Parents and guardians play a vital role in the success of our program. We ask all parents/guardians to:

  • Cheer positively for all players, on both teams.
  • Refrain from coaching, questioning officials, or criticizing players during games or practices.
  • Support coaching staff decisions regarding player development and playing time.
  • Model the sportsmanship values you want your child to display.
  • Communicate concerns respectfully and directly with club administration — not coaches during or immediately after games.

Coach Expectations

Infinity coaches are expected to:

  • Prioritize player safety, development, and well-being above winning.
  • Treat all players with fairness, dignity, and respect.
  • Communicate openly and honestly with players and families.
  • Model positive behaviour and sportsmanship at all times.
  • Follow all club policies and Basketball Alberta guidelines.

Consequences

Violations of the code of conduct may result in warnings, suspension, or removal from the program, at the discretion of the Infinity Basketball Club board. Repeated or serious violations — including harassment, abuse, or threats — will result in immediate removal with no refund.

Reporting Concerns

If you have a concern about conduct at any Infinity Basketball Club event, please contact the board directly at infinitylacrete@gmail.com. All concerns will be taken seriously and handled with discretion.
